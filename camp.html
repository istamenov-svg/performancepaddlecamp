The Performance Paddle System

What Makes This Camp Different

29 years of building a system that transforms how you play.

The Approach

Paddle Is Not Tennis on a Smaller Court

Most paddle instruction borrows from tennis — better strokes, harder shots, faster hands. That approach produces players who hit the ball well but don't understand the game they're playing.

Platform tennis is a fundamentally different sport. It's played in a cage, off screens, always in doubles. It demands its own strategy. The Performance Paddle system was built over two decades of teaching and competing at the highest levels for exactly this reason.

What You'll Learn

The Five Pillars

Court Positioning & Movement Patterns

Where you stand determines whether you win or lose the point before you hit the ball. We teach the exact positions for every game situation — serving, returning, net play, baseline defense — and drill them until they're instinctive.

Screen Play & Off-the-Wire Strategy

Reading the screen, setting up screen shots, and using the wires to create offensive opportunities. This is what separates paddle players from tennis players on a paddle court.

Communication & Partnership

Calling shots, switching, covering gaps, and building trust with your partner under pressure. We teach specific language and signals that our campers take back to their teams.

Shot Selection & Patterns of Play

When to drive, when to lob, when to go to the screen, and how to construct points that put pressure on the other team. We teach you to think 2–3 shots ahead in every rally.

Coached Match Play

Every session includes live match play with coaches on court, giving real-time feedback on your execution and decision-making. This is where the system comes together.

Camp Format

How Each Day Unfolds

Each session runs three days. Morning sessions focus on drill work — isolated skills practiced with repetition until they become reliable. Afternoon sessions shift to pattern play and coached matches where you apply what you learned in game situations.

Each day builds on the previous one. The coach-to-player ratio is kept deliberately low so you receive individual attention and specific feedback — not just general tips shouted across three courts.

Every season, Mike and Ana design a fresh format with a unique theme. Returning campers get new material. First-time campers get the foundation.

A Typical Day

Morning Session
Drill work — stroke mechanics, positioning, screen play. Isolated skills with repetition and individual coaching.

Break

Afternoon Session
